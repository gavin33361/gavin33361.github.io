Propositional Equivalence

Suppose that P and Q are propositional forms. We write P ≡ Q to mean that P and Q are logically equivalent, i.e., that P is true if and only if Q is true. Example:

p ∨ q ≡ q ∨ p

P ≡ Q is not a proposition. It is a statement about the propositions P and Q, namely that the propositions are true (and false) under the same conditions.
